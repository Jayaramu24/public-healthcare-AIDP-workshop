Business Context

MPHA turns healthcare operating pressure into trusted insights and guided action

Metro Public Health Authority needs to improve claims transparency, reduce denial leakage, monitor disbursements, track membership and provider accreditation, and turn policy guidance into faster operational decisions. This workshop shows how Oracle AI Data Platform, Autonomous AI Lakehouse, Oracle Analytics Cloud, machine learning, and AI agents work together to deliver that operating model.

Business problems

Why MPHA cares

Claims transparency and denial leakage

Leaders need to know which districts, programs, and claim types are driving denied claims.

Disbursement and payment visibility

Finance teams need to see where submitted amounts are not converting into paid disbursements.

Membership and provider accountability

Operations teams need to monitor coverage, accreditation, and service-area risk together.

Policy guidance is hard to operationalize

Claims reviewers need playbook guidance without manually searching long policy documents.

Insights and agent capabilities

What the platform enables

Claims Executive Overview

OAC shows KPIs, trends, denial hotspots, district views, claim-type analysis, and Assistant responses.

Spatial and facility access insight

Service-area and facility views help teams decide where operational pressure needs intervention.

Denial risk scoring

ML prioritizes claims that are more likely to be denied or require operational review.

Claims and Policy Copilot

AIDP agents combine SQL over the Claims star schema with RAG over the MPHA playbook.

What participants build in the workshop

The hands-on path turns the business context into working data, analytics, ML, and agent capabilities.

Raw healthcare data CSV, JSONL, GeoJSON, and document inputs land in Object Storage.
AIDP medallion layers Bronze and Silver datasets make the healthcare data governed and reusable.
AI Lakehouse star schema The Claims star schema becomes the trusted Gold serving layer.
OAC executive dashboard Participants build the MPHA Claims Command Center and validate Assistant prompts.
ML and agent extensions Optional labs add denial scoring, RAG knowledge base, and copilot orchestration.
