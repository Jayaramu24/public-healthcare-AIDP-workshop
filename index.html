Business use case

Prioritize care access, claims, disbursement, membership, provider oversight, staffing, outreach, and quality.

MPHA leadership needs a shared view of facility pressure, immunization equity, respiratory surveillance, claims, public-program payments, membership eligibility, provider accreditation, and quality events so teams can act before financial, access, or service risk escalates.

  • Identify facilities exceeding wait-time or occupancy targets.
  • Compare public-health pressure across districts and reporting weeks.
  • Track immunization campaign reach by district and age group.
  • Connect respiratory positivity trends to emergency demand.
  • Surface service-quality events that need faster closure.
  • Spot claims denial patterns and disbursement exceptions by coverage program.
  • Monitor membership renewal risk and high-risk member segments.
  • Review provider accreditation scores, corrective actions, and expiry risk.
Architecture

Medallion architecture from raw data to business insights.

Raw files land unchanged, AI Data Platform creates Bronze and Silver Delta layers, and the Gold business layer is set up in AI Lakehouse as a dimensional star schema for OAC dashboards and generative summaries.

Shared foundation: all participants land the same raw files in Bronze and derive the same conformed Silver tables first.
Instructor-led path: the facilitator builds the Claims star schema end to end, then shows how events, maps, and document intelligence enrich the OAC experience.
DIY path: participants apply the same pattern to the Facility Access Daily star schema using facility operations and population-health pressure context.

Why the shared Silver matters

Everyone completes the same Bronze and Silver work before the branch. That makes the instructor-led demo and the DIY challenge feel like one coherent pattern, not two different solutions.

  • Bronze preserves source structure, nested payloads, geometry, and document lineage.
  • Silver adds typed columns, conformed statuses, keys, and reusable healthcare measures.

Claims star schema as the teaching pattern

The Gold claims model is intentionally compact so the room can focus on business logic, star schema thinking, and how those measures surface in OAC.

  • Monthly aggregation makes the measures easy to explain and validate live.
  • The provided dashboard shows how a pure star schema can be enriched with map context, event context, and document-grounded assistant answers.

Facility Access Daily star schema as the proof point

The second Gold model lets participants prove they can repeat the pattern on their own while still benefiting from shared spatial, JSON, and document-grounded assets.

  • Spatial joins can reveal high-pressure districts and facility hotspots on a map.
  • Document chunks support an assistant flow for provider accreditation guidance and policy Q&A.
Workshop outline

Core labs with a guided Claims star schema and a participant-owned Facility Access challenge.

Lab 1 · 45 minutes

Create the public healthcare lakehouse

Prepare the landing zone, upload the compact raw dataset pack, and validate the workshop-ready source inventory.

  • Create the object storage and landing folder structure.
  • Upload five CSV files, one JSONL file, one GeoJSON file, and one DOCX playbook.
  • Validate file names, row counts, and source lineage tags.
Lab 2 · 75 minutes

Build Bronze and Silver in AIDP

Use Spark and Delta Lake to ingest raw data, type the records, conform references, and derive shared healthcare measures.

  • Create Bronze Delta tables for all raw sources.
  • Build Silver conformed tables with derived claims, access, and event metrics.
  • Prepare the shared foundation used by both Gold teaching paths.
Lab 3 · 60 minutes

Instructor-led Claims star schema and OAC workbook

Aggregate the shared Silver claims data into a Gold star schema in AI Lakehouse and build the provided Claims star schema workbook.

  • Create `mpha_fact_claims_monthly` and the four conformed claims dimensions.
  • Publish OAC-ready claims measures, filters, and prompts.
  • Walk through the reference workbook as the pattern participants will reuse later.
Lab 4 · 45 minutes

DIY Facility Access Daily star schema challenge

Participants apply the same Gold star schema modeling pattern to Facility Access Daily and design their own dashboard experience.

  • Create `mpha_fact_facility_access_daily` from the shared Silver facility tables.
  • Join directly to date, facility, district, and pressure-band dimensions.
  • Build a participant-owned dashboard without a prebuilt OAC page.
Optional Lab 5 · 45-60 minutes

Claims denial risk scoring

Use curated claims, event context, and provider-accreditation signals to score likely denial hotspots and prioritize manual review.

  • Derive ML-ready features from claims, event, and accreditation context.
  • Publish `denial_risk_score`, `likely_denial_bucket`, and `review_priority` outputs.
  • Show how scored slices extend the Claims star schema story in OAC.
Optional Lab 6 · 45-60 minutes

Claims and Policy Copilot

Build a copilot that combines SQL over the Claims star schema with grounded answers from the vectorized MPHA playbook.

  • Create a SQL tool over claims, disbursement, membership, and accreditation Gold objects.
  • Create a RAG tool over the document knowledge base for policy grounding.
  • Demonstrate a Claims and Policy Copilot flow for claims leaders and policy teams.
